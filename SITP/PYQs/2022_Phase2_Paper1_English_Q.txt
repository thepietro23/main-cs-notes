Q.2) 
Precis: Write a precis based on the below article in about 
200 words. Give a suitable title. (30 marks) 
Nutrition is a fundamental aspect of human life, impacting 
health, growth, and overall well-being. The food we consume 
plays a pivotal role in our nutrition, and understanding the 
challenges and opportunities in this domain is essential for 
addressing a wide array of health and societal issues.
In an age of abundance, nutrient deficiency remains a significant 
global challenge. Millions of people, especially in low-income 
countries, suffer from malnutrition, which includes both 
undernutrition and micronutrient deficiencies. Undernutrition 
can result in stunted growth, cognitive impairments, and 
increased vulnerability to diseases. On the other hand, 
micronutrient deficiencies, often referred to as “hidden hunger,” 
lead to severe health consequences. Iron-deficiency anemia, 
vitamin A deficiency, and iodine deficiency disorders are 
prevalent examples. Addressing these challenges requires not 
only increasing food availability but also ensuring that diets are 
diverse and nutrient-rich.
Paradoxically, alongside undernutrition, overnutrition is also 
a pressing issue. Obesity rates are skyrocketing worldwide, 
leading to a host of health problems such as heart disease, 
diabetes, and certain cancers. High-calorie, low-nutrient diets, 
sedentary lifestyles, and the abundance of processed foods 
contribute to this problem. Tackling overnutrition requires a 
comprehensive approach that includes education on healthy 
eating, policy changes to reduce the prevalence of unhealthy 
foods, and fostering an environment that promotes physical 
activity.
Ensuring food security, or the consistent availability and access 
to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food, is another significant 
challenge. Many regions of the world still struggle with food 
insecurity, while others face issues of food safety. The global 
food supply chain is intricate and prone to disruptions due to 
various factors, including climate change, political instability, 
and diseases like the recent COVID-19 pandemic. This 
necessitates a focus on building resilient and sustainable food 
systems to meet current and future demands.
The current agricultural practices are often resource-intensive, 
contributing to environmental degradation. They deplete soil 
quality, demand vast amounts of water, and rely on excessive 
use of fertilizers and pesticides. Sustainable agriculture, which 
balances the needs of today with the needs of the future, offers a 
solution. This approach prioritizes regenerative farming, organic 
practices, and the efficient use of resources, aligning food 
production with long-term ecological health.
A startling amount of food is wasted globally, from farm to 
plate. This includes crops that never make it to market, food 
discarded by consumers, and excess production. Food waste 
is not just an ethical concern when millions go hungry; it also 
contributes to environmental issues, as rotting food releases 
greenhouse gases. Reducing food waste involves changes at 
multiple levels, from improved harvesting and transportation 
practices to consumer awareness campaigns.
Advances in biotechnology and data analytics are enabling the 
field of personalized nutrition. Personalized nutrition tailors 
dietary recommendations to an individual’s unique genetic 
makeup, metabolism, and lifestyle. This approach has the 
potential to improve health outcomes and reduce the risk of diet-
related diseases. Apps and wearable devices are increasingly 
being used to gather data about individuals and offer customized 
dietary advice.
A shift toward plant-based diets offers multiple opportunities 
in food nutrition. Plant-based diets are not only associated 
with lower environmental impacts but also with various 
health benefits, such as reduced risk of heart disease and 
obesity. The development of plant-based meat alternatives and 
dairy substitutes provides consumers with more choices for 
sustainable and healthy diets.
The promotion of diverse and sustainable agricultural practices 
aligns with both the challenge of sustainable agriculture and the 
opportunity to enhance food nutrition. Regenerative agriculture 
and organic farming contribute to healthier soil and reduce the 
need for harmful chemicals, ultimately producing more nutrient-
rich crops. Sustainable practices can lead to healthier food 
options for consumers while preserving the environment for 
future generations.
The realm of food nutrition is marked by both challenges and 
opportunities. Nutrient deficiency, overnutrition, food security, 
sustainable agriculture, and food waste represent significant 
hurdles that need to be addressed. However, through advances 
in biotechnology, personalized nutrition, and the adoption 
of plant-based diets and sustainable agricultural practices,

Q.3) 
Reading Comprehension: Answer the following questions 
based on the below paragraph
The transformative impact of advanced weather forecasting 
technology on agriculture explores the importance of reliable 
weather forecasting for agriculturalists and why traditional 
weather predictions fall short of meeting the specific needs of 
farmers. Additionally, it delves into the sources used by modern 
weather forecasting apps to collect data and the factors that 
contribute to their accuracy.
Reliable weather forecasting is of paramount importance 
to agriculturalists as it directly influences the success and 
efficiency of farming operations. Farmers depend on weather 
forecasts to make informed decisions about when to plant, 
irrigate, harvest, and protect their crops from extreme weather 
conditions. Inaccurate predictions can lead to significant 
financial losses and crop damage. Traditional weather forecasts, 
while useful to a certain extent, may not provide the level of 
specificity and accuracy required by farmers. They often rely on 
generalized regional data and do not consider local variations 
in weather patterns, which can be crucial for crop management. 
Moreover, traditional forecasts typically offer limited lead 
time, making it challenging for farmers to plan their activities 
effectively.
To address the limitations of simple weather forecasting, 
modern agriculturalists turn to advanced weather forecasting 
apps that provide more precise and tailored information. These 
apps leverage a range of sources to collect data, including 
satellite imagery, ground-based weather stations, radar systems, 
and weather models. Satellite imagery offers a bird’s-eye 
view of weather patterns, allowing farmers to monitor cloud 
cover, precipitation, and temperature across their specific area. 
Ground-based weather stations provide real-time data on local 
conditions, while radar systems track the movement of storms 
and precipitation. Weather models use complex algorithms to 
analyze all these data sources, providing forecasts that consider 
multiple variables, such as wind patterns and atmospheric 
pressure. This multifaceted approach allows the app to gather a 
comprehensive view of the current weather situation.
The accuracy of these advanced weather forecasting apps can 
be attributed to their ability to collect and process a wealth of 
data from various sources. By combining information from 
satellites, weather stations, radar systems, and predictive 
models, these apps can generate highly detailed and location-
specific weather forecasts. The accuracy is further enhanced 
by the use of powerful computer algorithms that continuously 
analyze the data and update forecasts in real-time. This ensures 
that agriculturalists receive up-to-date and reliable information, 
enabling them to make critical decisions about their farming 
practices. In this way, technology has revolutionized weather 
forecasting in agriculture, providing farmers with a tool that is 
tailored to their specific needs and that significantly enhances 
the precision and efficiency of their operations.
