# 🔍 SEBI Grade A (IT) — PYQ Analysis & Insights

> Based on actually reading: 2022 & 2024 Phase-1 Paper-1 (common), 2022 Phase-2 Paper-1 (English descriptive), and the **official 2024 IT Phase-2 Paper-2 Information Handout**. Last updated 2026-06-29.

---

## 🅰️ Phase 2 · Paper 2 (IT) — the merit-decider (2/3 of Phase 2, 85% of final)

### Format flip-flop (VERY important)
| Cycle | Format | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **2024** | **Actual coding test** on "Parikshak" (write & submit working programs) | 3.5 hrs (30 min reading + 3 hrs coding) |
| **2025** | **MCQ** — logic-flow completion, debugging, syntax, dry-run output, data analysis | 180 min |

→ **SEBI changes this format between cycles. Prepare for BOTH** — be able to (a) write working code that passes test cases, AND (b) read/dry-run/debug code in MCQs.

### 2024 coding-test mechanics (from official handout)
- **9 questions:** 4 Simple (solve any 3 × 10 = 30) · 3 Medium (solve any 2 × 20 = 40) · 2 High (solve any 1 × 30 = 30) → **100 marks**.
- **Languages allowed:** C++ (GCC 9.3), Java 1.8, Python 3.5. *(Pick one and get fast in it.)*
- **Grading = input/output based**, ≥5 test cases/question. **Partial marks** — proportional to test cases passed (need ≥2 "Y" to score; e.g., 3/6 cases = ½ marks). Best submission counts.
- **Strategy:** solve Simple-first (easy 30), attempt every question (partial marks reward even incomplete logic), choice within each tier means you can skip your weakest.
- **Syllabus:** Data Structures **40** (Array, LinkedList, Stack, Queue, Trees/BST, Heap, Hashing, Matrix, **JSON objects**) · Algorithms **30** (sort/search, greedy, DP, backtracking, divide&conquer, pattern searching) · OOP **20** · String Manipulation **10**.

> **Action:** Solve 150–200 problems on a judge (HackerRank/LeetCode/GfG) across DS+Algo in ONE language. This single block earns the most marks in the whole exam.

## 🅱️ Phase 1 · Paper 1 (common, qualifying) — observed structure
80 Q = **GA (Q1–20) · English (Q21–40) · Quant (Q41–60) · Reasoning (Q61–80)**. Each 20Q/25M. Difficulty: **moderate-to-hard, in-depth** (not surface GK).

### General Awareness (Q1–20) — high effort needed
- **Dominantly current affairs (last ~6 months before exam)**: appointments, important days, schemes (PM Gati Shakti, Atal Innovation Mission), Budget & Economic Survey, sports (AFCON, Winter Olympics, Asian C'ships), indices (Henley Passport).
- **Strong financial/securities-market tilt** → SEBI committees (e.g., ALeRTS), **CDSL/BSE/depositories**, BIS, insurance companies, RBI/IRDAI campaigns. *This overlaps with the interview too.*
- Questions are **specific & deep** (not "capital of X") — need consistent CA notes.

### English (Q21–40) — toughest verbal among banking exams
- Reading Comprehension, **word-swap & error spotting, advanced sentence correction (GMAT-like grammar), para-jumbles, cloze/fill-in**. Passages are abstract/economic.

### Quantitative Aptitude (Q41–60) — DI-heavy
- **~50% Data Interpretation** (caselet, table, ratio-based multi-step DI). Plus number series, quadratic-comparison (x vs y), arithmetic word problems (boats, SI+CI combo, time&work, partnership, mixture). Calculation-intensive.

### Reasoning (Q61–80) — puzzle-heavy
- **~60% puzzles & arrangements** (linear with unknown count, circular+attribute, month/date scheduling), plus coding-decoding, ranking/inequality, word-formation, strong/weak arguments, conclusions.

> **For a CSE candidate:** Quant & Reasoning logic is familiar — but SEBI's **DI + puzzle density and time pressure** need practice. GA is the real grind → start a daily current-affairs + financial-awareness habit now.

## 🅲️ Phase 2 · Paper 1 (Descriptive English, 1/3 of Phase 2, 30% cutoff)
From 2022: **Essay (300w, choose 1 of 4) + Precis (200w + title) + Reading Comprehension (descriptive answers).**
- 2022 essay choices: bank's role in financial literacy · mental health · techno-stress in education · **ARCs (Asset Reconstruction Companies)**.
- Precis topic: nutrition/food systems. RC: weather-forecasting tech in agriculture.
- **Insight:** essays mix **finance/economy + social/tech** — a finance-aware, well-structured writer scores. Practice 1 essay + 1 precis weekly (daily from Month 5).

## 🎯 Net strategy (priority order by marks-impact)
1. **Phase 2 IT Paper 2 (DS+Algo+OOP coding/MCQ)** — biggest merit lever. Daily problem-solving in one language. ← *your DSA-Notes & OOPs folders feed this.*
2. **Phase 1 IT Paper 2 (Programming 30% + rest)** — must clear 40% comfortably; programming is the anchor.
3. **Descriptive English (Phase 2 Paper 1)** — don't ignore; 1/3 weight, 30% cutoff. Weekly essay/precis.
4. **Phase 1 Paper 1 common** — qualifying only; keep GA (financial/SEBI) ticking daily + steady DI/puzzle practice.

> Phase 1 just needs to be *cleared*. Rank is built in **Phase 2**, and within it the **IT technical paper dominates**.
